Overview of Aravind Campus

All Aravind centres offer training programmes; hence they are well equipped to facilitate learning. For those seeking to acquire clinical skills, in addition to well-equipped classrooms, there are adequate facilities for direct and experiential learning. Most diagnostic and surgical equipment such as slit lamps, indirect ophthalmoscopes, operating microscopes, and laboratory microscopes have observerscopes, video or still cameras attached. At Madurai, 3-dimensional video recording and playback system allows surgical trainees to get an enhanced realistic view of the procedures.

Surgical training is imparted in special training wet lab using animal eyes with specific teaching microscopes. Wet labs are equipped with the state-of-the-art simulators and have a significant collection of surgical videos.

Classrooms

All Aravind classrooms are comfortable and well-equipped with advanced teaching technology and enable interaction with the faculty. The classrooms at LAICO allow for flexible layouts, group discussions, case presentations and active interaction. Audience response systems have been introduced to enable participant feedback, opinion gathering and instant training evaluation.

Online resources

Aurosiksha is a recent online teaching portal that holds presentations by experts. Aurosiksha’s lecture series are a collection of journal club presentations and guest lectures.

Kolam is an online discussion forum that allows trainees and alumni to stay connected. It allows sharing of resources, interaction with faculty and fellow participants.

Grand Rounds

Aravind Grand Rounds is a regular weekly feature. Cutting edge teleconference facilities allow all the Aravind Centres to be connected. This draws excellent presentations from Aravind trainees and regularly features guest lectures from experts invited from around the world.

Library

The libraries boast of a comprehensive collection of books, journals and other media on all specialties of the eye as well as management and community ophthalmology. Besides this, the library offers excellent user support which enables trainees and researchers access to relevant information through a web based platform.

While at Aravind

The Aravind Institutions strive to be patient-centric and the organization has developed a strong culture of discipline and compassion. Trainees at Aravind are required to follow a simple dress code.

  • Women: It is appropriate for women to wear long skirts or full pants and loose fitting tops. Sleeveless tops are not appropriate. Please do not wear tight leggings or jeans and skimpy tops to work. Those who are staying for longer periods can buy Indian clothes such as the salwar kameez, as these will be more appropriate and comfortable.
  • Men: Here the men mostly wear button-down shirts and loose trousers. Avoid wearing jeans, t-shirts or shorts to work.
  • Shoes: Sandals or other loose shoes would be comfortable. Most visitors wear socks for fear of foot problems. Flip-flops are useful and can be purchased locally at a cheap rate.

We recommend that all trainees take a look at Vanakkam – a guidebook to South India and Aravind. As you prepare for your travel to India and to the Aravind destinations, ‘Vanakkam’ will provide you with detailed information on what to expect when you arrive. This includes travel arrangements, living conditions, local weather and local food, as well as information on sightseeing and restaurants in the cities where the Aravind facilities are located.

Each Aravind campus has a unique way to take time off once in a while to bring all employees and trainees from different cadres together through competitions to showcase different talents, celebrating local festivals, cultural evenings, motivational lectures etc.

For International Trainee's

International Trainees

All foreign trainees are required to get a Visa to enter India. Please go through the https://indianvisaonline.gov.in/visa/index.html that will help you prepare for a hassle-free time here. Foreign trainees may be required to register with the local police upon arrival. Contact your course coordinator to assist you in this.

Trainee Accommodation

On site accommodation facilities, with catering services, are available within the campus at all our centres. The types of facilities offered are different at each centre. PG Resident Quarters are available at all centres for the trainees. Short term trainees to Madurai can opt to stay at Inspiration - the international trainees’ hostel.
